About me

A career in programming was the logical next step in a lifetime of tinkering, discovery and creative problem solving. As a native North Carolinian, I was drawn to the Triangle. Raleigh/Durham is a vibrant place to live with countless opportunities, exciting tech start-ups, and of course a fantastic and growing food scene.

Working in technical support at ShopBot Tools allowed me to explore my passion for programming and taught me valuable skills. As a programmer, my greatest assets include my ability to keep calm under pressure, troubleshoot and find solutions to complex and layered problems, often with incomplete information.

I have worked at Duke University Medical Center in the Duke Cancer Institute as a Scientific Programmer on high throughput genomics data as a part of the Bioinformatics Shared Resource for more than four years. I develop preprocessing and analysis pipelines for genomics data and implement new statistical methods for cancer genomics. I am using C++, Python(3), and R most of the time. I also do some scientific cloud computing, mainly on AWS with EC2 or Kubernetes/EKS. I work with a diverse team of bioinformaticians and often work directly with clients throughout the Cancer Institute.
